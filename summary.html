Topic: Cookies & Sessions

Steps 3–14 Summary

Cookies: Track visit count, last visit, and first visit using browser storage.

Sessions: Store login state securely on the server using $_SESSION.

Login Flow: User submits password → validated → session created → access granted.

Critical Thinking Summary

Cookies vs Sessions: Cookies are client-side and less secure, sessions are server-side and safer.

Security: Real systems require hashed passwords, HTTPS, and protection against attacks.

Real World Use: Websites use cookies for login persistence, tracking, and personalization.

Conclusion

This lab demonstrated how cookies and sessions work together to track users and maintain authentication across multiple pages.
